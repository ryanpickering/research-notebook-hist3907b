The Society for the Conversion and Re- 
ligious Instruction and Education op the 
Negro Slaves in the British West India 
Islands was founded in the year 1794 by the 
exertions of the Right Rev. Beilby Porteus, 
D.D. Lord Bishop of London, upon a chari- 
table bequest of that eminent Philosopher and 
true Christian, the Hon. Robert Boyle. It 
was incorporated by Charter from his late Ma- 
jesty ; and since that period has steadily and 
diligently pursued its benevolent object as far 
as its means have allowed. 

In an age when the public feeling is so 
effectually awakened to the great Christian 
duty of diffusing the knowledge of true Reli- 
gion amongst those who yet " sit in darkness 
and the shadow of death," much need not be 
said in behalf of an Institution, which under- 
takes the work of imparting to so many thou- 
sands of the most ignorant of our fellow-crea- 
tures the saving truths and holy precepts of 
the Gospel. 

The Society has placed Chaplains and Cate- 



chists in many of the West India Islands. In 
the course of the last year it sent out eight 
Chaplains and three Catechists ; and the whole 
number of those who are at present supported 
in their ministry from the Society's funds is 
seventeen. It is gratifying to add, that the 
reception of those labourers in this good work, 
and the success of their ministry are of the 
most encouraging description. 

But while " the harvest is plenteous, the 
labourers are few." The spiritual wants of the 
West India Islands are of the most pressing 
kind. Many pious and able young men have 
offered themselves as Chaplains : but the So- 
ciety, having already pledged itself to an 
amount far exceeding its resources, is unable 
to avail itself of their services. 

This brief and simple statement, it is hoped, 
will be sufficient to establish the Society's 
claims on the support of a Christian Public : 
particularly when it is considered, that its 
Chaplains and Catechists will henceforth, in 
the discharge of their arduous duties, be sub- 
ject to the control, guided by the advice, and 
animated by the encouragement of the Bishops 
of the West Indian Church.

present resources ; by which it will be seen
